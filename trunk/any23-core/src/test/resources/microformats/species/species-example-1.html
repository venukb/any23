County Lists for the Region, 2004

Introduction

The following table shows all species in categories A to C of The British List  that have been reliably recorded in the area covered by the current boundaries of the four counties of our region, since records were kept. The regional total as at 31 December 2004 was 341 (see 'Changes'). The year shown is the latest in which the species has been observed in each county.

Subspecies or races are shown in {curly brackets} and are not included in the totals. "nk" refers to reliable, but undated, 19th–century records. A record shown as "<1836" occurred before that year.

We would welcome details of birds you have seen in the four counties of the West Midland region.

Notes

  • The county totals excludes all known escapes.
  • The records of Red Grouse and Black Grouse on the West Midlands list refer to Sutton Park which, at the time of the records, was part of Warwickshire.

Changes

  • The 1953 Staffordshire Tawny Pipit was rejected by BBRC and the species is thus removed from the list.
  • Ruddy Shelduck has been moved to Appendix 1 and therefore is removed from the list.
  • Bufflehead and Black-headed Bunting new to Region.
  • Warwickshire: Bufflehead, Black-headed Bunting (increase of 2 but loss of Ruddy Shelduck = 304)
  • Worcestershire: None (loss of Ruddy Shelduck = 296)
  • Staffordshire: Bufflehead, Broad-billed Sandpiper, Red-rumped Swallow (increase of 3 but loss of Ruddy Shelduck and Tawny Pipit = 309)
  • West Midlands: Bufflehead, Spotted Sandpiper, Little Bunting (increase of 3 and loss of Ruddy Shelduck = 269)
